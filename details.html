Consolidated Event Information

Town Council Proposal Details

This page centralizes venue and community-context details for the inaugural Asian American Youth Art Competition and showcase proposal.

Venue Request and Showcase Plan

Venue Request

Asian Blooming Artists proposed hosting the exhibition at a Town of Cary facility, with Cary Town Hall identified as the preferred exhibition location during AAPI Heritage Month in May.

Competition Scope

The inaugural competition collects work from Asian American youth ages 15-19 across North Carolina, including students from Cary, Wake County Public Schools, and surrounding communities.

Artwork and Judging

The theme is peace. Accepted media include paintings, watercolors, and small sculptures. A professional judging committee selects 30-50 finalists, followed by public voting for winner tiers.

Why This Matters

Community Representation

Asian Americans represent more than 25% of Cary's population. The event is designed to amplify youth voices and artistic perspectives in a visible, civic setting.

AAPI Heritage Month

Scheduling the showcase in May aligns with AAPI Heritage Month and highlights how Asian American cultures enrich the local identity of an inclusive community.

Public Participation

Public voting invites residents to engage directly with youth artwork while building understanding, recognition, and cross-cultural connection through art.

Operations and Annual Vision

Volunteer Staffing

The nonprofit's organizing members handle setup and deinstallation of exhibition materials, reducing operational burden on facility staff.

Organizing Team

The event is led by a dedicated youth-led nonprofit team coordinating submissions, curation, communications, and exhibit logistics.

Long-Term Goal

The proposal aims to establish the showcase as an annual program that encourages youth expression, strengthens artistic literacy, and deepens community understanding.
