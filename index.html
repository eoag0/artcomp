About Asian Blooming Artists

Founded by Carter Yao, a North Carolina high school student, Asian Blooming Artists is a nonprofit-led initiative focused on youth expression, cultural understanding, and community connection through visual art.

Mission

To create meaningful platforms where Asian American youth can share their stories, strengthen artistic literacy, and inspire civic dialogue through creativity.

Core Values

  • Representation of diverse Asian American voices
  • Community participation through accessible public voting
  • Respect, peace, and cross-cultural understanding
  • Youth leadership and volunteer-driven service

Nonprofit Status

This event is organized by the nonprofit Asian Blooming Artists with a dedicated volunteer team handling setup, curation, and deinstallation to minimize staff burden.

Inaugural Asian American Youth Art Competition

Open to Asian American youth across North Carolina from all school types.

Who

Asian American youth artists, ages 15-19, from communities and schools statewide.

What

Original paintings, watercolors, and small sculptures centered on the competition theme: Peace.

How

A professional jury selects finalists (30-50 works), followed by public voting to determine multiple award tiers.

Why

Asian Americans make up more than 25% of Cary's population. This showcase amplifies youth voices through art during AAPI Heritage Month and invites the public to participate in community-centered cultural celebration.

Event Timeline

  1. Spring

    Submission portal opens for statewide youth entries.

  2. Late Spring

    Professional judging committee reviews and selects finalists.

  3. May (AAPI Heritage Month)

    Public exhibition and community voting at the host venue.

  4. Post-Showcase

    Winners announced, archive published, planning begins for annual return.
